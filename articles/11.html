Indians Among 66 Evacuated From Conflict-Hit Sudan To Saudi

Saudi Arabia recently announced that it will safely evacuate Indians from Sudan for free. This was after India's Foreign Minister, S Jaishankar, made a call for evacuation of citizens from India. He had urged the need for safe and immediate evacuation.

Saudi Arabia responded to his call and announced the evacuations in a tweet from its foreign ministry. According to the tweet, Saudi Arabia would provide flight facilities for Indian citizens in Sudan “out of brotherly duty”. This would ensure their safe return to India.

India appreciated the move of assistance by Saudi Arabia and thanked them through twitter. They also thanked other countries such as Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and Pakistan who have helped facilitate these evacuations as well.

In conclusion, Saudi Arabia has announced that they will help evacuate Indian citizens from Sudan to India for free as part of “brotherly duty” after India's Foreign Minister S Jaishankar made a call for evacuation of citizens from India into safety. The country has earned appreciation from India for this kind gesture along with Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and Pakistan who have helped facilitate this operation as well.
