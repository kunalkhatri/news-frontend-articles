"Price For Speaking Truth": Rahul Gandhi After Vacating Delhi Bungalow

Rahul Gandhi, a popular Indian politician and the leader of the Indian National Congress, has vacated his bungalow in the national capital Delhi. He had been living there for nearly 15 years. His move has been met with a mixed reaction - some people appreciate him for taking this step and standing up for truth, while others feel that his decision was a 'price he had to pay for speaking truth'.

Mr Gandhi vacated his official residence just days after losing a three-way electoral showdown between Prime Minister Narendra Modi's Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), the Congress and Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) in the recently concluded elections. He had promised before the polls that he would vacate his government house if his party suffered defeat.
