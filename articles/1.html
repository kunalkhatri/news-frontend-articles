Fugitive Preacher Amritpal Singh Surrenders In Punjab, Arrested

Fugitive preacher Amritpal Singh has been detained by the police in Punjab for surrendering himself. Singh, a 30-year-old religious leader from Faridkot, had been on the run for the past few days, after being accused of delivering hate speeches at his events. The police had issued a lookout notice in order to apprehend him. He was found near Moga city and was detained by the Punjab Police on Wednesday.

On Monday, Singh had gone away without informing anyone, leaving behind messages saying that he wanted to end his life due to the harassment he was facing from the police and other authorities. The police trackers were able to locate him in Moga city where he had gone to meet some people. After being detained, Singh was sent directly to a temporary detention facility where his mental health evaluation is currently being carried out before further proceeding.

Amritpal Singh is a spiritual leader who had been giving speeches at various religious gatherings in Punjab. However, many of these speeches have alleged hate content which has led to investigation from authorities culminating in putting out a lookout notice for him.. The police have also seized his belongings including mobile phones after his surrender for further investigation into the content of his sermons and about his whereabouts during this period of disappearance.
