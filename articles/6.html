Indians Among 66 Evacuated From Conflict-Hit Sudan To Saudi

Saudi Arabia has announced that it is helping to evacuate Indians from Sudan, a neighbouring country. This comes shortly after a call from India’s External Affairs Minister, S. Jaishankar, for the safe return of Indians from Sudan.

Sharing the news on Twitter, Saudi Arabia said that they had worked with India to evacuate its citizens safely from Sudan. It added that it had acted in compliance with social distancing and safety protocols against the coronavirus pandemic which have been in place in the Kingdom since March.

The ministry of external affairs had on June 14 taken up the matter with Saudi authorities to arrange evacuation flights for Indians stuck in Sudan. The Indian embassy in Khartoum was in contact with those who wished to return home and Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) Spokesperson Anurag Srivastava later thanked Riyadh for its efforts in this regard.

Saudi Arabia has recently come to India’s aid during coronavirus pandemic by helping evacuate stranded passengers from Kerala last month and sending necessary medical supplies to our country.
